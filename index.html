$ introduction

I am a Master's student in Computer Science at Texas A&M University, specializing in advanced areas of Deep Learning under the guidance of Prof. Cheng Zhang. My research expertise and interests are uniquely positioned at the intersection of Embodied AI, Generative Models, and Human-Centric Computer Vision. My academic and industrial experience is dedicated to translating cutting-edge techniques, such as 3D Gaussian Splatting and Vision Transformers, into high-impact, generalized AI solutions across diverse domains, from autonomous systems to clinical diagnostics.

My core research vision is twofold, focusing first on creating truly intelligent and capable agents. My primary work centers on developing generalized AI models for Egocentric driven scene understanding. This involves creating end-to-end frameworks that utilize first-person views to predict fine-grained affordance—what actions an agent can take with an object—and establish effective scene navigation strategies. This foundational work aims to empower intelligent agents to successfully execute complex, long-horizon tasks in real-world environments.

Complementing my work in Embodied AI, I am deeply passionate about applying advanced Diffusion-based Generative models to critical problems in Biomedical Analysis. Specifically, my focus is on leveraging these powerful models for cardiac motion analysis from echocardiogram images. My contributions in this area, including the development of algorithms for synthetic data generation and precise 3D heart mesh reconstruction, are driven by the goal of developing accurate, non-invasive solutions for disease prediction and functional assessment, directly benefiting clinical diagnostics.
